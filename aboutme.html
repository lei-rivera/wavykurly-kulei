About the Creator, Lei

When I was young, I had short, extremely curly hair. It was enough of a defining factor in my looks for my family to give me the nickname "Kuleilei" or "Kulei", with 'Kulei' being a combination of my name and the tagalog word 'kulot', which means 'curly'.

Despite my family genetically having curly hair, no one knew how to take care of curly hair, especially my mother. She grew up chemically straightening hers (likely a result of the beauty standards in the Philippines), so that's how she cared for her daughters' hair.

Like my sisters, I grew up treating my hair as 'straight'. But I would get frustrated when my 'straight' hair would become hard to manage; it would get poofy if I blow dried it, it'd frizz up easily, and layers made first-grade me look like Dora the Explorer. At some point I woke up with such rough hair my grandmother jokingly called me "bruha", which in Tagalog means "witch" (and was borrowed from the spanish "bruja", like many other tagalog words).
